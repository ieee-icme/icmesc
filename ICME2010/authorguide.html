Author Guidelines

1. General Information

ICME 2010 paper submission and review process is conducted in the following manner:

  • ICME 2010 requires authors to submit the title, list of authors, a brief abstract of about 150 words, and a list of 3-6 keywords for each paper two weeks before the paper submission deadline (Abstracts Due: December 18, 2009). Authors can update the above information when submitting the full-length paper. (New!)
  • The length of each paper should not be longer than 6 pages following the format given in the paper templates. (New!)
  • The ICME2010 review process is Double Blind. Both authors and reviewers do not know each others’ identities and affiliations. Please refer to the paper templates and "Section 3: Paper Format, Submission and Review" for detailed instructions on how to preserve anonymity. (New!)
  • Submission of the paper abstract and the full-length paper must be done electronically.
  • After submitting the paper abstract, a Paper ID will be assigned. In the full-length paper for review, authors must indicate this Paper ID as listed in the templates. (New!)
  • Paper submissions will be reviewed by experts in their respective research fields selected by the conference committee. Authors will be notified of the review results by email.
  • Once your paper has been accepted, a final camera-ready version of the paper incorporating any revision based on the feedback from the reviewers must be submitted. The camera-ready paper must be submitted in a publishable form before the submission deadline given below.

2. Requirements

  • Papers should be no longer than 6 pages, including all text, figures, and references. The paper MUST be formatted according to the Paper Templates given in Microsoft Word or LaTeX. Details of the format requirements can be found in the templates.
  • Papers must be submitted before the deadline for review.
  • Accepted papers must be presented in the conference venue according to the technical program. Failure to do so will result in exclusion of the paper in the conference proceedings and IEEE Xplore Digital Library.
  • At least one of the authors MUST register for the conference at one of the non-student rates offered. He/She MUST register before the deadline given for author registration. Failure to register before the deadline will result in automatic withdrawal of your paper from the conference proceedings and program.

3. Paper Format, Submission and Review

3.1 File format

  • All submissions must be in PDF.
  • All fonts and subsets must be embedded. Times-Roman font is strongly encouraged.
  • The filename of the review paper should be the four-digit Paper ID followed by the file extension (.pdf), for example, “1234.pdf” (New!)
  • Detailed examples about the paper format using LaTeX and Microsoft Word are available in the paper templates.

3.2 Electronic paper submission

  • Go to the paper submission link on ICME 2010 home page.
  • Complete the online submission form. Make sure all the authors’ names are in the same order as appear on the final camera-ready PDF.
  • Authors will be asked to indicate their affiliated society (if applicable) from one of the four sponsoring societies.

3.3 Supplementary Materials

  • Supplementary materials could be uploaded to support your paper. You can do so immediately after submitting the full paper for review. To do this, go back to the “Author’s console” and a link will be provided for you to upload your supplementary material.
  • Allowable file types include DivX, doc, docx, PDF and wav with a maximum file size of 30 MB.

3.4 Double-blind review (New!)

  • ICME 2010 adopts a double-blind review process. Please strictly adhere to the rules about author name(s) and affiliation(s), citation(s) and reference(s) indicated in the templates.
  • The full-length papers for review must contain no information that may identify the authors or their affiliations. Please use “Anonymous ICME 2010 submission” and the Paper ID in place of authors’ names and affiliations. Violation of this requirement will lead to automatic rejection without review.
  • Citation of your own work is allowed but this have to be done with care in order not to disclose your own identity. Please refer to the manuscript template for more information and example.
  • Only when your paper has been accepted will you be allowed to provide names and affiliations under the title.

3.5 Online review process

  • All submitted papers will go through a visual inspection by our staff before going to review process.
  • All submissions will be rated by a reviewer committee selected by conference committee according to quality, relevance and correctness.
  • Authors will be informed about the reviewers’ comments and acceptance decision for their papers by email.

3.6 Dual/double submissions

  • The author(s) should ensure that the submitted paper has not been published or accepted to publish in substantially similar form.
  • The author(s) should ensure that no paper with substantial overlapping is under review or going to be submitted by any other conference, workshop or journal during the review process of ICME 2010.
  • The author(s) should ensure that no multiple papers with substantial overlapping are submitted to ICME 2010 as well.

3.7 Copyright issue

  • Once the paper has been accepted, the authors must complete and submit the IEEE copyright form to IEEE.
  • Failure to do so will render the paper inadmissible for publication in IEEE Xplore, and the paper will subsequently be removed from the proceedings.
