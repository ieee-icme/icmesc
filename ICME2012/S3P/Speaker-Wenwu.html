Title:Social Media Cloud Computing and Social Media Recommendation

Abstract:

Today's Internet has witnessed the rapid convergence of online social network service and online multimedia service - a massive number of heterogeneous multimedia contents are shared among users in the online social network. The unique patterns of how multimedia contents are generated, distributed and consumed, and the large-scale, dynamic and complex social network topology impose great challenges for social multimedia applications. To realize the potential of social multimedia, these challenges need to be addressed. This talk first presents the concept and framework of social multimedia cloud computing, and then discusses the design space of the infrastructure/platform services and social multimedia applications. Last, we will use social media recommendation as an application example to present interest-oriented social media recommendation and influence-oriented social media recommendation.

About the Speaker:

Wenwu Zhu is with Computer Science Department of Tsinghua University as "1000 People Plan" Professor. Prior to his current post, he was a Senior Researcher and Research Manager at Microsoft Research Asia. He was the Chief Scientist and Director at Intel Research China from 2004 to 2008. He worked at Bell Labs at New Jersey as Member of Technical Staff during 1996-1999.

Wenwu Zhu is a Fellow of the IEEE. He has published over 200 referred papers in the areas of (Internet & wireless) multimedia communications and networking. He is inventor or co-inventor of over 40 patents. His current research interests include multimedia cloud computing, social media, and multimedia communications and networking.

He served on various editorial boards of IEEE journals, such as Guest Editor for the Proceedings of the IEEE, IEEE JSAC, IEEE T-CSVT, and IEEE Wireless Communication Magazine; Associate Editor for IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing, IEEE Transactions on Multimedia, and IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology. He received the Best Paper Award from IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology in 2001 from IEEE Circuits and Systems Society and the Best Paper Award from Multimedia Communication Technical Committee (MMTC) of IEEE Communications Society in 2004. He is the past Chair of Visual Signal Processing and Communication Technical Committee of IEEE Circuits and Systems Society (2006-2008), and served in the Steering Committee of IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing (2007-2010). He currently serves as an Associate Editor for IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology and IEEE Transactions on Multimedia, the Chair of Beijing Chapter of IEEE Circuits and System Society, and advisory board of International Journal of Handheld Computing Research. He will serve as TPC Co-Chair of IEEE ISCAS 2013.

Wenwu Zhu received the B.E. and M.E. degrees from National University of Science and Technology, China, in 1985 and 1988, respectively, the M.S. degree from Illinois Institute of Technology, Chicago, and the Ph.D. degree from Polytechnic Institute of New York University, New York, in 1993 and 1996, respectively, in Electrical and Computer Engineering.
