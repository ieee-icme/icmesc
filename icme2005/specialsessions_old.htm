Call for special sessions

The organizing committee of ICME2005 is welcoming proposals for special sessions during the conference.  Special sessions will be an integral part of the conference. Below you will find details on what special sessions are, how to organize them, how to submit proposal and the deadlines.

What are special sessions?

A special session is focused on a particular topic in the multimedia processing field.  It is organized by a renowned scientist who selects potential contributors.  Special sessions begin with an introduction to the session topic by the session organizer, making it easier and more attractive for the audience to attend the subsequent talks. All proposed contributions are submitted to the general ICME paper review process and will be rated according to their scientific value. All contributing papers will be included in the published conference proceedings.

How to organize a special session?

This is expected from organizers:

  • pick a topic for a special session that would attract large interest in the multimedia processing field and for which you can come up with an outstanding team of contributors;
  • contact about 3-4 potential authors, specialists in the field and invite them to submit a paper for the conference which is tuned to your session;
  • organizers are advised to write an introductory paper for the proceedings on the topic of their session; the paper should be submitted via the regular submission procedure and is going to be reviewed in the general ICME reviewing process; for the introductory paper there is no requirement for the incorporation of original research results;
  • make sure that each paper is properly evaluated before submission;
  • in case of acceptance, to decide on the final contents, order and length of presentations, in agreement with the review committee recommendations;
  • to chair the session during the conference;
  • to present an introduction (typically 30 minutes) at the beginning of the session.  This should both introduce the topic to non-specialists, and make the link between the state-of-the-art and the papers presented in the session.

The following aspects should be taken into account:

  • The page limit for ICME2005 papers will also apply to the special session papers, but only as an average.  It is up to the organizer to assign a page limit to each individual contribution. The size of introductory papers is therefore not necessarily limited to 4 pages.
  • An entire session will take no more than 1,5 -2 hours
  • A contribution invited by a session organizer will NOT be automatically accepted for ICME2005.   But: papers accepted by the general ICME2005 program committee will be assigned a slot in the general ICME2005 program, even if they were meant to  contribute to a special session which fails to be accepted. 
  • In case of acceptation of a session proposal, all session contributors will have to register to the conference and pay the registration fee. 

How to submit a proposal to organize a special session?

Each session contribution is submitted as a separate paper by the author(s). See the submission page for more details. In addition, send an e-mail to the special session committee with:

  • the title of the session
  • the titles and author names for the papers contributing to your session, including your own introductory paper
  • a brief description (± 10 lines), to be published on the ICME2005 Web site in case of acceptance
  • your affiliation, address, phone and fax number, e-mail address
  • a description of your expertise in the field of the session (or a link to your personal Web page where this information can be found)

 Deadlines

  • January 18, 2005 (10 am CET): submission of proposals for a special session
  • March 31, 2005: notification of acceptance
  • April 30, 2005: deadline for submission of camera-ready full versions of the papers
