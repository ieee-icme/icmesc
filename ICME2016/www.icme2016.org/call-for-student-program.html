Call for Student Program


The Organizing Committee of the IEEE International Conference on Multimedia and Expo (ICME) 2016 is pleased to announce the following student-centered events that will take place during the conference:
  1. Student-Industry Luncheon
  2. Three Minute Thesis (3MT) competition
Both events are free to graduate students who have registered for the main conference or workshops. The luncheon provides a venue for students to meet and interact with industry partners and explore future collaboration or possible employment prospects. To participate in the luncheon, please submit the following documents via CMT [https://cmt.research.microsoft.com/ICMEW2016] in the “Student Program Track” as a single PDF file:
  1. Your CV (maximum 2 pages), which should include your contact details
  2. A letter from your Advisor confirming your student status
The CVs of the participants will be made available to the industry partners in order to facilitate interaction and networking during the luncheon.

The 3MT is an interesting yet challenging way to present a graduate research thesis to a general audience in just three minutes using a single PowerPoint slide! 3MT was first established at the University of Queensland in 2008 and has since been held at many universities around the world. For further information please visit http://threeminutethesis.org Awards will be given to the best 3MT presentations at ICME 2016. To participate in the 3MT competition, please submit the following documents via CMT [https://cmt.research.microsoft.com/ICMEW2016] in the “Student Program Track” as a single PDF file:
  1. An abstract of your thesis (maximum 1 page)
  2. Your CV (maximum 2 pages), which should include your contact details
  3. A letter from your Advisor confirming your student status
Note: all applicants to the 3MT competition will automatically be registered for the luncheon as well, so there is no need to submit separate applications.

Application deadline for both the luncheon and 3MT is May 13, 2016.

If you have any questions, please e-mail Student Program Co-Chairs Z. Jane Wang (zjanew@ece.ubc.ca) and Ivan V. Bajić (ibajic@ensc.sfu.ca).
