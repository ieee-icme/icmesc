A Tutorial on Nonnegative Matrix Factorisation with Applications to Audiovisual Content Analysis

Abstract 
Nonnegative Matrix Factorisation (NMF) is a powerful non-supervised data decomposition framework, akin to a latent variable analysis process, which has proven successful in a variety of real-world applications, including image analysis, video summarization, text mining, finance, bioinformatics, electroencephalographic signal analysis, etc. While it has been receiving a booming interest in the audio research community where it is a state-of-the-art technique for tasks such as source separation or music transcription, it remains to date rarely used in the wider multimedia community, especially as far as video content analysis is concerned, despite a few remarkable contributions.

Hence, this tutorial aims at introducing the NMF framework to researchers in multimedia signal processing and content analysis, providing the theoretical foundations, methods and algorithms for NMF-based modelling, while focusing on the aspects that are particularly relevant to audiovisual content analysis.

On the methodological level, the model will be discussed in the light of its geometric interpretation, which is key to understanding the ill-posedness of the original formulation and the need for regularization. The algorithms for learning the model will be presented based on general cost functions (i.e. β-divergences) and motivated as either gradient-descent or majorisation- minimisation procedures. We will also dive into constrained NMF schemes, considering various types of constraints, namely sparsity, component-shape, temporal smoothness and cross-modal constraints. A special emphasis will be put on the latter as tensor factorisation and co-factorisation schemes are addressed, allowing for the joint processing of two or more modalities, typically audio and visual streams. Subsequently, a geometric approach will be introduced that allows for performing kernel- based factorisations, hence accounting for non-linearities in the data.

Two main application scenarios will be considered: unsupervised video structuring, especially onscreen person spotting and multimodal speaker diarization (exploiting both the audio and visual streams), and audio source separation where demos of so-called informed (i.e. informed by some other data or user input) NMF-based source separation approaches will be presented.
